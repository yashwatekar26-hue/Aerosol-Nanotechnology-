Paper 1 · Review

AJP Technology: The Foundation

Wilkinson et al. (2019) — Int J Adv Manuf Technol

📄 Read Abstract

"Aerosol Jet Printing (AJP) is an emerging contactless direct write approach… Freeform deposition, coupled with a relatively large stand-off distance, enables increased geometric complexity. Wide material compatibility, high resolution and independence of orientation provide novelty… This overview summarises the principles, reviews applications and discusses hurdles to industry adoption."

AJP Process Schematic

① Atomization (liquid → aerosol) · ② Size selection & focusing · ③ Deposition with sheath gas collimation

Two Atomization Routes

Ultrasonic (1–10 cP, monodisperse)
Pneumatic (1–1000 cP, polydisperse, requires virtual impactor)

Extreme Material Versatility

Silver nanoparticles · Ceramics · SU‑8 photoresist · Proteins · DNA — all printable with the same system.

Key Challenges

Overspray (satellite droplets) degrades line quality.
Ink formulation is empirical; no universal "Z‑number" exists.

Reported finest lines: ~10 µm (Cai et al.). Resistances ~2× bulk silver. Bending endurance < 10 000 cycles.

Paper 2 · Innovation

Solving the 60‑Year Dispersion Problem

Hwang et al. (2024) — Nano Energy

📄 Read Abstract

"Polymer-based nanocomposites emerged in the 1960s… however, achieving uniform nanomaterial dispersion has impeded progress. Here, we present a polymer-based nanocomposite with highly dispersed nanomaterials, achieved through aerosol-jet-based multi-material three-dimensional (AM3D) printing. Numerical simulations facilitate the avoidance of interfacial compatibility issues… the 3D structured nanocomposite exhibits a uniform dielectric constant and low dielectric loss."

Nanomaterial Dispersion: Old vs. New

❌ Pre‑Mixing (Agglomeration)

Nanoparticles clump together → weak spots, electrical leakage

✅ Separate Atomization (AM3D)

Perfect dispersion – uniform properties across the film

Left: agglomeration from conventional pre‑mixing. Right: AM3D’s separate atomization ensures pristine dispersion.

The "Graffiti" Trick

Inspired by graffiti art: atomize polymer and nanoparticles separately, combine only at the printhead. No pre‑mixing → no agglomeration.

16× Dielectric Enhancement

Single‑layer ε′ rose from 14 (pure PVDF‑TrFE) to 28. Multi‑layer sandwiches achieved 16× higher ε′ with low loss tangent.

TENG Output Boost

Open‑circuit voltage increased from ~80 V (pure) to ~90 V for optimized nanocomposite.

Paper 3 · Breakthrough

Electric Field Guided 3D Nanoprinting

Jung et al. (2019) — Science (preprint style)

📄 Read Abstract

"We propose a novel 3D nano-printing concept… We apply a floating dielectric mask to focus charged aerosols through a convergent electrostatic field… we found two different regimes (3D growth mode and 3D writing mode) exist depending on the translation velocity of the stage."

Electrostatic Focusing Architecture

x) Growing 3D Nanopillar Positive ion cushion
Ion‑induced electrostatic lens focuses charged nanoparticles through mask holes, building high‑aspect‑ratio pillars.

Electrostatic "Funnel"

Ions accumulate on the mask, creating a local field that guides nanoparticles with sub‑100 nm precision – no physical nozzle needed.

Two Modes: Grow or Write

Growth: slow stage → pillars, helices. Writing: fast stage → brush‑like deposition (letters "3D").

100× Smaller

Resolution < 100 nm vs. conventional AJP’s ~10 µm – a leap into true 3D nano‑architectures.
