NARRATIVE INTRODUCTION // 0:00 - 2:00

The Alchemy of Aerosols:
3D Nanoprinting with Invisible Mist

Traditional additive manufacturing is fundamentally bound by physical boundaries. Extrusion heads must physically contact flat layers, and high-viscosity structural elements restrict geometric variation.

Aerosol Jet Printing (AJP) bypasses this baseline completely. By atomizing fluid ink suspensions into suspended clouds of flying nanoparticles, we can guide material from a distance to grow high-resolution functional structures directly onto highly irregular, curved, or flexible objects.

CORE PHYSICS // 2:00 - 4:30

A Review of Aerosol Jet Printing: Fundamentals & Flight

Wilkinson & Smith, 2019 — Micro-Manufacturing Systems

This work breaks down the core mechanics of particle aerosol generation. Liquid ink stocks are fractured into micro-droplets using either Ultrasonic Atomizers (ideal for homogeneous, low-viscosity solutions) or Pneumatic Atomizers (capable of handling heavy material loads).

The decisive breakthrough lies in the Coaxial Deposition Head. A secondary shield stream of gas wraps symmetrically around the airborne aerosol column, pinching it into a highly focused stream to prevent nozzle clogs while sustaining printing accuracy across long standoff distances.

MATERIAL COMPOSITION // 4:30 - 6:30

Highly Dispersed Nanomaterials via Mid-Air Aerosol Blending

Hwang et al., 2026 — Multi-Material Engineering Journal

When distinct nanomaterials are pre-mixed inside common ink reservoirs, severe material failures occur: particle surfaces form clusters, causing massive agglomeration loops that block structural output channels.

Hwang et al. introduced a dynamic process: keeping disparate functional matrices separate until flight. By isolating components into discrete aerosol chambers and blending them inside the airborne stream line milliseconds before impacting the landing site, perfect composition dispersion is maintained.

EXTREME SCALE ACCURACY // 6:30 - 8:30

Three-Dimensional Nanoprinting via Charged Aerosol Focusing

Seoul National University Research Initiative

To reach true micro-to-nano architectural layout fidelity, mechanical gas shrouds are augmented by electromagnetic control forces. This methodology applies explicit electrostatic polarization directly to the airborne particles.

By casting these charged mist clouds across an overhead floating dielectric mask layer, the electric field line distribution converges into localized focal coordinates. This lets you stack individual nanometer particles perfectly on top of one another to construct vertical 3D micro-pillars.
